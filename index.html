Web hacking

june 8

Web hacking involves exploiting vulnerabilities in websites and web applications to gain unauthorized access or manipulate data. Techniques like SQL injection and XSS are commonly used. Consequences include data breaches and website defacement. Mitigate risks with robust security measures, regular vulnerability scanning, and user education on safe browsing habits. Stay vigilant to protect against evolving web hacking threats.

Desktop hacking

june 8

Desktop hacking involves unauthorized access and manipulation of computer systems primarily operating on desktop platforms. Hackers employ techniques like malware, phishing, and social engineering to compromise system security and access sensitive data. The consequences can be severe, including identity theft and financial loss. Strong cybersecurity measures, such as strong passwords and regular updates, along with user education, are crucial for protecting against desktop hacking threats. Stay vigilant and practice safe online habits to minimize the risk of falling victim to desktop hacking.

Android hacking

june 8

Android hacking involves exploiting vulnerabilities in the Android operating system and apps to gain unauthorized access or extract sensitive information. Techniques include malware, phishing, and rooting. To protect against Android hacking, be cautious with app downloads, keep devices updated, and use reputable antivirus software. Avoid unsecured Wi-Fi networks and practice good security habits to minimize the risk of falling victim to Android hacking.
